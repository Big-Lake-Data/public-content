Illinois Ridehail Market Summary

Economic scale of driver earnings and customer fares

Author

Matt Schumwinger, Big Lake Data

Published

January 30, 2026

This brief uses 2024 and 2025 data to understand the size of the Illinois ridehail market and the flow of revenue between riders, platforms, and drivers as observed in per-trip fares and pay. The estimates provide a baseline for assessing the economic implications of AV displacement, especially the potential scale of lost driver earnings.

Summary of findings

The economic footprint of Uber and Lyft ridehail services in Illinois is large. On a statewide basis, we estimate:

  • $2.5 Billion in driver earnings annually.

  • $3.6 Billion in customer fares paid annually.

  • Per-trip averages: customer fare $21.59; driver earnings $14.77 (incl. $1.40 tips).

Methodology

This analysis estimates annual aggregate driver earnings and related market totals for Illinois ridehail by combining (1) per-trip averages computed from trip-level Gridwise Analytics data and (2) a statewide trip count that’s benchmarked to 2025 data from the City of Chicago (Chicago Data Portal: Transportation Network Providers - Trips).

  • Sample definition – The Gridwise trip sample consists of 1,102,606 Uber and Lyft trips starting or ending in Illinois, contributed by 4,316 unique drivers. Sample months are May 2024, August 2024, November 2024, and February 2025 (the middle month of each quarter). We treat per-trip averages from the sampled months as representative and apply them to an annual statewide trip count benchmarked to Chicago’s 2025 totals.

  • Per-trip measures – We compute mean values for driver earnings, driver tips, customer fare, trip distance, and trip duration. Trips with no tips are treated as $0 (rather than missing) for tip-related calculations.

  • Scaling from Chicago to Illinois – The total Illinois trip estimate is benchmarked on City of Chicago public data. Chicago reports 93.5M Transportation Network Provider (TNP) trips in 2025.1 In the Gridwise sample, 55.4% of trips have either a pickup or dropoff within Chicago city limits. We estimate statewide trips as: Illinois trips = Chicago trips ÷ Chicago share = 93.5M ÷ 0.554 ≈ 168.8M.

  • What’s included in fares? Customer fare reflects the total amount the customer was charged in-app for the trip (before tips and after promotions).

  • Conservative assumptions – driver gross pay doesn’t include non-trip earnings, such as bonuses, incentives, “top-ups”, and other pay adjustments. In prior years, we have found this to comprise a small, yet significant portion of total driver earnings. In this regard, the analysis likely understates true aggregate driver earnings. Because non-trip earnings are not included, total driver earnings reported here should be interpreted as a lower bound.

Massachusetts Ridehail Market Summary

Economic scale of driver earnings and customer fares

Author

Matt Schumwinger, Big Lake Data

Published

January 24, 2026

This brief uses newly complete 2025 data to estimate the size of the Massachusetts ridehail market and the flow of revenue between riders, platforms, and drivers. The estimates provide a baseline for assessing the economic implications of AV displacement, especially the potential scale of lost driver earnings.

Summary of findings

The economic scale of Uber and Lyft ridehail services in Massachusetts is immense.

  • In total, we estimate that Massachusetts Uber and Lyft drivers earn $1.6 Billion annually (including $145 Million in tips).
  • Average customer fare per trip: $21.38; average driver earnings per trip: $15.69 (including $1.35 in tips).
  • Implied platform take rate is 32.9%, corresponding to $755 Million in platform take on $2.3 Billion in total fares.

Methodology

This analysis estimates statewide aggregate driver earnings and related market totals for Massachusetts ridehail in 2025 by combining (1) per-trip averages computed from trip-level Gridwise Analytics data and (2) a 2024 statewide trip count baseline from the Massachusetts Department of Public Utilities (DPU 2024 rideshare data report).

  • Gridwise trip sample consists of 723,713 Uber and Lyft trips starting or ending in Massachusetts, contributed by 2,399 unique drivers. The trip sample includes the second month of each quarter: February, May, August, and November 2025.

  • Per-trip measures: We compute mean values for driver earnings, driver tips, customer fare, trip distance, and trip duration. Trips with no tips are treated as $0 for tip-related calculations; missing values in other numeric fields are ignored in means (na.rm = TRUE).

  • Platform take: The implied platform take rate is calculated as (avg customer fare − avg driver gross pay) / avg customer fare, where gross pay is defined as driver earnings minus tips. Total platform take equals total fares multiplied by this take rate.

  • Conservative pay estimate: driver gross pay doesn’t include non-trip earnings, such as bonuses, incentives, “top-ups”, and other pay adjustments). In prior years, we have found this to comprise a small, yet significant portion of total driver earnings. Thus, this analysis likely underestimates true aggregate driver earnings.

  • Statewide scaling: Massachusetts DPU has not yet released 2025 ridehail trip totals. To estimate 2025 statewide volume, we extend the DPU time series using the most recent observed year-over-year change in Massachusetts ride volume (2023 to 2024, which was +15.5%). This Massachusetts-specific growth rate is a conservative assumption and is broadly consistent with the 18% year-over-year trip growth Uber reported company-wide in 2025. Applying +15.5% to the 2024 DPU total yields a projected 104,991,233 trips in 2025. Statewide totals are calculated by multiplying this 2025 trip estimate by the corresponding per-trip averages.
